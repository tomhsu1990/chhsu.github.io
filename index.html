Selected Works

Soft Subdivision Search
Rod and Ring Robots

We keep pushing the configuration space limitation of our Soft Subdivision Search (SSS) in Motion Planning problem. Based on our twin foundations of ε-exactness and soft predicates, our motion planner can solve the problem in R³ x S² configuration space. The technical innovations include subdivision atlases for S², introduction of Σ2 representations for footprints, and extensions of our feature-based technique for “opening up the blackbox of collision detection”.

Soft Subdivision Search
Non-crossing 2-links Robot

We extend our Soft Subdivision Search (SSS) planner to solve Path Planning problem of thick non-crossing 2-link robots. Based on our SSS framework, by introducing geometric soft predicates for thick non-crossing 2-link robots, our SSS planner can tell whether there is a path or not in realtime. In addition, we compare our planner with the state-of-art sampling methods like PRM, Toggle PRM, Lazy Toggle PRM, RRT, and RRT-Connect. Generally, our SSS planner has a better performance than those sampling planners.

Motion Segmentation
with a Moving RGB-D Camera

Motion segmentation serves as an important role in many robotics perception problems such as moving object tracking, simultaneous localization and mapping ... etc. Based on this motivation, we proposed a fused motion segmentation in a highly dynamic scenario with a moving RGB-D camera.
