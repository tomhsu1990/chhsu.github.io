Motion and Semantic Segmentation

advised by: Prof. Chieh-Chih (Bob) Wang

    In robotics scenarios, there is a number of applications using data from a moving camera, for instance: navigation, tracking, and localization ... etc. It is inevitable to encounter multiple moving objects. Therefore, segmenting motion becomes an essential problem to enhance the performance of these general tasks. Our goal aims to partition regions in one RGB-D image with same estimated motion in 6 DoF. Moreover, we combined the idea of physical support to have a better understanding of the motion segments.

Motion and Semantic Segmentation in Crowded Urban Areas with a Moving RGB-D Camera


By utilizing a RGB-D structured light camera, we propose a novel and robust algorithm to segment motions from consecutive frames. Based on a modified random sample consensus algorithm (RANSAC), we exploit the coherence of color and spatiality in the scene to estimate motion. It inherits the computational efficiency and probabilistic robustness from the RANSAC paradigm. After aligning two point clouds by the estimated transformation, we use inlier and outlier distribution as the prior knowledge to conduct graph-cut based segmentation. Moreover, we introduce physical support to realize the relation between motion segments. We provide a large annotated RGB-D dataset captured in a crowded urban environment to demonstrate our idea. Comparing to several motion segmentation methods in the same pipeline, we show that our approach performs well in the highly dynamic scene.

Ching-Hsiang Hsu, "Graph-cut based Motion Segmentation with Physical Support Relationships in Crowded Urban Areas from a Moving RGB-D Camera," National Taiwan University Thesis Press, July, 2014.

Interactive Image Segmentation

In the motion segmentation section, two important techniques to do segmentation are graph-cut optimization and simple linear iterative clustering (SLIC). I have tried these two methods in interactive image segmentation first. The instruction is to use red/blue pen to annotate your interested/uninterested region. Then, through maxima-flow algorithm, the region could be separated accordingly. It's simple to implement and the performance is quite good.
